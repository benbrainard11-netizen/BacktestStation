### Phase 2 — tighter diagnostic matching (`2d2904a`)

Original ±10-min first-touch match undercounted: a port setup touched at 09:58 UTC, terminally rejected as `touch_too_old`, then re-touched at 13:32 UTC and FILLED at 13:33 was scored as "none" because its first_touch was 3.5h before the live entry. Now the tracing subclass tracks:
- `last_touch_ts` (overwrite per re-touch)
- `filled_at_bar_ts`
- `n_transient_waits` and `n_terminal_rejections` separately

And `_matching_setup` runs four tiers (highest score wins):
- 3 = FILLED within 10 min
- 2 = last_touch within 10 min
- 1 = first_touch within 10 min
- 0 = first_touch within 60 min ("saw it, didn't fire")

After the upgrade, 4 of 6 live trades on 04-22/04-23 score 3.

### Phase 3 — the 04-24 silence (`5a73f7d`)

Spent ~30 minutes investigating "no port setups on 2026-04-24" before realizing `read_bars` treats `end` as **partition-exclusive** — `--end 2026-04-24` was loading 04-22 + 04-23 only, never 04-24. The CLI's `--end` is inclusive in user-mental-model so I made the script translate to read_bars' form via `end + 1 day`.

With `--end 2026-04-25` (or now `--end 2026-04-24` with the inclusive fix):
- Port emits **6 trades** on the live window (matches live's 6 by count)
- 5 of 6 score 3 (FILLED within 10 min)
- 1 of 6 scores 0 (port saw same direction at a different zone, touched pre-RTH and stale by entry window — genuine signal-detection difference, not an orchestration bug)
